الأنساب الطالبيّة

الأنساب الطالبية هي سلاسل النسب المتصلة بالإمام علي بن أبي طالب رضي الله عنه، وهي أنساب عريقة توارثتها الأجيال جيلاً بعد جيل، وحفظتها كتب الأنساب والمشيخات.

فروع النسب الطالبي

يتفرع النسب الطالبي إلى عدة فروع رئيسية، أشهرها:

  1. الحسنيون: نسبة إلى الإمام الحسن بن علي رضي الله عنهما
  2. الحسينيون: نسبة إلى الإمام الحسين بن علي رضي الله عنهما
  3. العقيليون: نسبة إلى عقيل بن أبي طالب
  4. الجعفريون: نسبة إلى جعفر بن أبي طالب

The Ṭālibī Lineages

The Ṭālibī lineages are genealogical chains connected to Imam Ali ibn Abi Talib (may Allah be pleased with him). These are ancient lineages passed down through generations and preserved in genealogical books and records.

Branches of the Ṭālibī Lineage

The Ṭālibī lineage branches into several main divisions, most notably:

  1. The Hasanids: Attributed to Imam al-Hasan ibn Ali (may Allah be pleased with them both)
  2. The Husaynids: Attributed to Imam al-Husayn ibn Ali (may Allah be pleased with them both)
  3. The Aqilids: Attributed to Aqil ibn Abi Talib
  4. The Ja'farids: Attributed to Ja'far ibn Abi Talib

Family Trees

The Genealogy Committee of the Union works on documenting and preserving the family trees of Hashemites in Sudan, and providing a digital database for these genealogies.

History of the Ashrāf in Sudan

The migration of Hashemites to Sudan began many centuries ago and occurred in successive phases. They came from the Hijaz, Egypt, the Maghreb, and Abyssinia.

Historical Phases

  1. Pre-Islamic Period in Sudan:

    There were trade relations and historical connections between the Arabian Peninsula and Sudan before Islam.

  2. The Introduction of Islam to Sudan:

    The migrations of Muslim Arabs, including some Hashemites, to Sudan began in the early centuries of Islamic conquest.

  3. Islamic Kingdoms Period:

    This period witnessed the settlement of many Hashemite families in various regions of Sudan, and the emergence of their role in spreading Islam and knowledge.

  4. Mahdist Period and Turkish Rule:

    Hashemites played a prominent role in religious and social life during this period.

  5. Independence Period and Beyond:

    The influence of Hashemite families continued in the cultural, religious, and social life of contemporary Sudan.

Contributions of Hashemites in Sudan

Hashemites had a significant role in:

  • Spreading knowledge and Islamic culture
  • Establishing mosques and Quranic schools
  • Social and educational reform
  • Leading Sufi movements and religious orders

The term "The Ninety Families" refers to the main Hashemite families known in Sudan, whose lineages have been documented and certified by the Genealogy Committee of the Union.

Geographical Distribution of Hashemite Families

Hashemite families are spread across various regions of Sudan, with certain branches concentrated in specific areas such as:

  • Northern region and the Nile
  • Gezira and White Nile
  • Kordofan and Darfur
  • Eastern Sudan
  • Khartoum and its suburbs
